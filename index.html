hw-03: Practicing D3

This webpage displays two types of visualizations: a grouped bar chart and a scatter plot. Both visuals use distinct data sets to analyze a correlation between their respective independent variables.

Your first visualization goes here:

This is our first visualization, a scatter plot. The dataset's elements were selected from a random set of data points we encountered online. The dataset examines whether there exists a correleation between the number of plants and fish in a certain pond. Since the number of fish and plants seem pretty uncorrelated on the surface and the objective is to discover whether there exists some correlation, we felt that a scatterplot would best serve our purpose. A scatter plot plots two independent attributes against each other, which is exactly what we need to do in order to spot a potential correlation.

Your second visualization goes here:

This is our second visualization, a grouped bar chart. The dataset describes restaurants in Boston through features like a rating and their distance from Northeastern's campus. We chose to use this dataset because the data is original and relevant to ourselves. A grouped bar chart is an appropriate encoding because it groups data by each restaurant, and is able to use sub-groups, individual bars, to represent the two features. The two features are also differentiated by color; red bars show the rating and blue bars show their distance.
